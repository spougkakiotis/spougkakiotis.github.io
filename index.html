About Me

My professional interests lie in the field of optimization, and computational mathematics. My research is focused on the analysis and development of novel and efficient algorithms for the solution of nonlinear (possibly non-smooth and/or stochastic) optimization problems.

Apart from my studies, I enjoy spending my time playing and composing jazz and contemporary music, focusing on the electrical or upright bass and the classical piano.

Roughly speaking, my research time is divided as shown below.

Analysis and Implementation of Optimization Algorithms

30%

Optimization for Operational Research and Physical Systems

25%

Risk-constrained Learning for Trustworthy Decision Making

20%

Numerical and Matrix Analysis

15%

Learning Abstract Mathematics, Logic and Philosophy

10%

Education

  • 2021 - PhD in Operational Research and Optimization

    I completed my PhD at the University of Edinburgh. My research was focused on optimization, and more specifically, developing robust and efficient algorithms for the solution of convex optimization problems. In particular, I worked on the theoretical analysis as well as implementation of Interior Point Methods, Proximal Point Methods, Alternating Direction Methods of Multipliers, and their combinations.
    University - University of Edinburgh, School of Mathematics
    First Supervisor - Prof. Jacek Gondzio
    Second Supervisor - Dr. John Pearson
    Duration - 3.5 years [Sep. 2017 - Sep. 2020, Jul. 2021 - Dec. 2021]
    Location - Edinburgh, United Kingdom

  • 2017 - MSc in Operational Research with Computational Optimization

    I completed an MSc on Computational Optimization at the University of Edinburgh. Some of the courses attended were:
    - Large Scale Optimization for Data Science,
    - Modern Optimization Methods for Big Data,
    - Non-linear Optimization,
    - Stochastic Optimization,
    among others. My dissertation thesis was concerned with the implementation of a Dual Regularized Interior Point Method (IPM), providing either exact or inexact solutions.
